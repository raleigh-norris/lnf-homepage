Frequently asked questions

We currently serve the Bay Area, Los Angeles, and Seattle, with Austin and Washington D.C. coming soon. Our team and network of specialists are expanding to new markets regularly, so check back if you don't see your city yet.

Our pricing depends on the scope of your project and the services you need. Get in touch with our team for a free consultation, and we'll walk you through the costs based on whether you're buying a ready-made compound or building from scratch.

Multi-unit properties, homes with ADU potential, large lots with room to build, and properties in zones that allow additional dwelling units all make great candidates. The best compounds tend to balance private living spaces with shared outdoor areas and common spaces.

Key factors include zoning regulations, the number of separate living units (or the potential to add them), shared outdoor space, and proximity to amenities. It's also important to consider the legal structure — how ownership, costs, and decision-making will be shared among your group.

Compound living gives you the best of both worlds: the privacy of your own home with the built-in community of your closest friends and family next door. It means spontaneous dinners, shared childcare, lower costs through shared resources, and the deep sense of belonging that comes from having your people nearby.

Compound purchases often involve multiple buyers, unique financing structures, and properties that don't fit neatly into traditional real estate categories. You'll need specialists who understand co-ownership agreements, multi-unit zoning, and group financing — which is exactly what our network provides.
