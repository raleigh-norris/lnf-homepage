Frequently asked questions

Living near friends means intentionally buying homes or units close to one another—often on the same property—so you can share daily life while maintaining private space.

Live Near Friends helps people buy and build duplexes, triplexes, fourplexes, and multi-home properties designed for this lifestyle.

You can buy property with friends or family by:

  • Purchasing a duplex, triplex, or fourplex
  • Creating a Tenancy in Common (TIC) agreement
  • Forming an LLC for shared ownership
  • Applying for a joint mortgage
  • Drafting a co-ownership agreement that outlines responsibilities and exit terms

Clear legal and financial planning is essential for long-term success.

A family compound is a property with multiple homes or units that allow extended family members to live independently while staying close together.

Common setups include:

  • A main home plus an ADU
  • A duplex or triplex
  • Multiple homes on one lot
  • Large properties eligible for subdivision

Family compounds are increasingly popular as housing costs rise and families look for built-in support systems.

In some cases, buyers form an LLC or use fractional ownership structures depending on lender requirements and state laws.

Live Near Friends typically earns compensation through standard real estate transaction commissions, similar to traditional buyer representation.

For most buyers, there is no separate upfront fee to explore properties or begin conversations. Costs and commission structures vary by market and transaction type, and transparency is provided early in the process.

The most common property types include:

  • Duplexes
  • Triplexes
  • Fourplexes
  • Homes with ADUs (Accessory Dwelling Units)
  • Adjacent homes purchased together

These options allow shared land while preserving private living spaces.

Live Near Friends helps groups of friends or family:

  1. Clarify their goals and ownership preferences
  2. Identify properties suited for shared living
  3. Understand co-ownership and financing options
  4. Connect with experienced real estate and legal professionals
  5. Navigate the purchase process as a coordinated group
  6. Renovate or build properties for multiplayer living

The platform is designed specifically for people who want to buy property together—not just find a house.

Traditional agents typically focus on one buyer or household at a time. Live Near Friends’ network of expert real estate agents specializes in multi-party purchases and intentional proximity living.

That means guidance on:

  • Co-ownership structures
  • Group decision-making
  • Property types suited for shared living
  • Long-term planning and exit strategies

The focus isn’t just buying a home, but building a sustainable, friend-centered living arrangement.
